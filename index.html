Citations

Akhenaten

Freud, S. (1939). Moses and monotheism. London: Hogarth Press. (In this book, Freud discusses the similarities between Akhenaten and Moses, arguing that Moses may have been an adherent of the religion established by Akhenaten).

Nefertiti

Redford, D. B. (1984). Akhenaten: The heretic king. Princeton: Princeton University Press. (This book discusses the religious reforms of Akhenaten and their potential influence on the development of monotheism, including the religion of Moses).

Moses

Assmann, J. (1997). Moses the Egyptian: The memory of Egypt in Western monotheism. Cambridge, MA: Harvard University Press. (This book explores the ways in which Egyptian culture and religion may have influenced the development of Jewish and Christian monotheism, including the possible influence of Akhenaten on Moses).

The Aten

Osman, A. (2002). Moses and Akhenaten: The secret history of Egypt at the time of the Exodus. Rochester, VT: Bear & Company. (In this book, Osman argues that Moses was actually a follower of Akhenaten and that the biblical account of the Exodus is based on historical events that occurred in Egypt during Akhenaten's reign).

The Amarna Letters

Goldwasser, O. (2006). How similar were the biblical and Egyptian texts describing the exodus? In Exodus und Eisodus: Komposition, Chronologie und Rezeption (pp. 9-20). Berlin: De Gruyter. (This article discusses the similarities between the biblical account of the Exodus and Egyptian texts, including those from the reign of Akhenaten).

Monotheism

Kessler, R. (2017). A comparative study of the religious reforms of Akhenaten and Josiah. Journal of Biblical and Theological Studies, 2(2), 73-91. (This article compares the religious reforms of Akhenaten and Josiah, a king of Judah who implemented religious reforms in the 7th century BCE, and discusses the possible influence of Akhenaten's reforms on the development of Jewish monotheism, including the religion of Moses).
